About Couple & Family Therapy

Dana utilises an approach to her work referred to as Systemic Psychotherapy which seeks to explore the patterns and connections between people in relationships. As we are shaped by and in the relationships we live in, understanding them better helps us to determine how we can live with others in ways which enrich and inspire. Systemic psychotherapy offers a way of doing therapy with our 'significant others', spouses or partners, children, parents or siblings, in order to build the capacity of the relationship itself to withstand the inevitable pressures and transitions all relationships must face.

While it is preferable to work with all those involved within the 'system in focus', it can also be effective to seek systemic therapy as an individual or partial family to explore and address relational unwanted relational patterns.

There is no set number of sessions and no optimum number; clients determine how long they will attend. They last for one hour and frequency of sessions is flexible according to client preference and availability.
